ABOUT THE FILMMAKER

I'm a script-slinging storyteller with a Southern soul — part Shonda, part Spike, part Santa Claus. From gritty crime dramas set in Atlanta's underworld to heartwarming holiday comedies featuring viral TikToks and dollar-store wreaths, I navigate genres like a showrunner in sneakers. I craft worlds where FBI agents clash with cartel bosses, artists lead double lives, and Grandma might just become the next social media sensation. With a producer's mind, a writer's wit, and a director's eye for both tension and tenderness, my scripts are not just narratives but living, breathing embodiments of culture, conflict, and comedy.

Every project, whether it's The Line, Granny's House, LifeArt, or The Twelve Dates of Christmas Tree Lot, pulses with purpose. I've been fortunate to contribute to over 30 projects and 186 episodes of "One Tree Hill", honing skills in creativity, leadership, critical thinking, problem-solving, time management, and multitasking. My knack for clear communication has been instrumental in bridging the gap between producers and department heads, ensuring seamless production workflows.

MY DEVELOPMENT PROCESS

Every story I tell follows a bold, intentional path — from spark to screen.

Development

Laying the foundation

Concept Creation
Writers brainstorm and develop the core idea that drives the story.

Scriptwriting
The screenplay is drafted, refined, and polished for production-readiness.

Financing
Producers secure funding through investors, studio partners, or grants.

Pre-Production

Prepping for the big picture

Planning
A detailed schedule and production budget are mapped out.

Casting
Actors are auditioned and selected to embody key roles.

Location Scouting
Filming environments are identified, scouted, and secured.

Crew Assembly
Key team members — directors, cinematographers, designers — are hired and briefed.

Production

Bringing the vision to life

Filming
Cameras roll on set, on location, or in studio.

Daily Reviews
Footage is reviewed each day to ensure quality, continuity, and performance.

Adjustments
Creative and logistical tweaks are made in real time as the story unfolds.
